From the first consultation to patient lifetime value

1,419 patients, 17,294 appointments, 20 months. The story isn't how many come in, but how many make the leap from a general consultation to a specialization — the real revenue engine.

01

The pulse of the business

Acquisition is heavily front-loaded: almost every patient arrives in May 2024, and from there monthly volume declines month after month. Specialized appointments are a small but persistent fraction of the total.

02

Who converts?

Of every 100 patients, 95 reach a general consultation but only 30 reach a specialization. That narrowing is the central business question: the remaining 70% never cross into the higher-value service line.

03

What is a patient worth?

Average LTV breaks down into two service lines. The general consultation provides the base; specialization adds the rest — but only for those who convert. Each component is explained by its drivers (visits × ticket).

04

Do they stay?

Acquisition was essentially a one-off: 1,382 of the 1,419 patients have their first visit in May 2024. On that dominant cohort, retention holds ~72% activity at month one and falls below 25% by month six. The bleed is fast and early.

05

Who pays the bills?

There's concentration, but it's no textbook 80/20: the top 20% of patients hold 61% of revenue, and you need 38% to reach 80%. The revenue base is more spread out than the cliché suggests.

06

Six patients, up close

The first visit of the target patients. None cost $50: the "fixed intake" assumption from the original analysis doesn't survive the data — first consultations range from $158 to $205.

07

Methodology & assumptions

  • Pipeline: raw CSV → raw table (NVARCHAR) → light star schema core (fact + dimensions) → analysis views → static JSON. All in SQL Server.
  • LTV: total revenue / unique patients, decomposed additively into general + specialized component.
  • Business assumption (unverified): Type 1 = general consultation, Type 2 = specialization. The dataset's origin is unknown; treat as a stated hypothesis.
  • Window: Mar 29, 2024 to Nov 30, 2025. Projection through Dec 31, 2026.

Source: patient financial dataset (17,294 appointments). Portfolio piece · data analysis.
